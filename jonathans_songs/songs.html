You made it to the main event: Welcome to my compositions!

Not everything I’ve made is in here yet, and I may update it from time to time. I started composing music in my downtime, just a few minutes a night, when I was living in South Korea; serving as a full-time missionary for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. It was difficult at first because I was relatively new at music, I had no access to instruments in the apartment, and I didn’t have a lot of time to work on them. I would have to compose my ideas on physical music score, then on p-days, my one day off in the week—typically Monday, I would use the computer we had access to in the church to transcribe my music digitally to an online composition web app called flat.io to see if it sounded like I wanted it to. Nowadays, I use Musescore, an open-source application that is more capable in my opinion. If you want to see the pdf of any of these motifs, you will be able to find it under the same title in my pdf download page. Again, I ask that none of my pieces be ripped or plagiarized for use in any sort of media, but feel free to play with my ideas and contact me with what you make!
